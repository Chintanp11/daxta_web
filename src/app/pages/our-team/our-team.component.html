Layla Sump

Ph.D., BCBA-D, LBA(Layla Abby)

Co-Founder

Layla Sump is a board certified behaviour analyst – doctoral. Layla is the CoFounder of DAXTA and leads the clinical efforts of the company. Dr. Sump is originally from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, but lives in the United States since 2003. Layla obtained her undergraduate and masters degrees in Psychology at Jacksonville State University, under the mentorship of Paige McKerchar, Ph.D., BCBA-D and her Ph.D. in Special Education from Texas Tech University, under the mentorship of David Richman, Ph.D., BCBA-D. Following her doctorate, Layla was a post-doctoral fellow at Trumpet Behavioural Health (TBH) under the mentorship of Linda LeBlanc, Ph.D., BCBA-D. Then, while at TBH, Dr. Sump served as Director of the San Francisco office, Director of Training, and Director of Clinical Services. Currently, Dr. Sump serves as a consultant for autism service providers and she is an Adjunct Professor for Pepperdine University.

Dr. Sump has published in several peer-review scientific journals including Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis, Behavior Analysis in Practice, Research in Developmental Disabilities, Journal of Intellectual Disability Research, Journal of Developmental and Physical Disabilities, and Journal of Mental Health Research in Intellectual Disabilities. Dr. Sump has presented her research at national and international conferences and has been a guest reviewer for the Journal of Applied Behavior Analysis, Behavior Analysis in Practice, The Analysis of Verbal Behavior, and Journal of Behavioral Education.

Gabriella Ulloa

(M.A., BCBA, QBA)

Co-Founder

Gabriella Ulloa's a board certified behaviour analyst with eight years of experience working in the field of applied behaviour analysis. She joined DAXTA’s team in December of 2019 as a Clinical Specialist. Her current responsibilities include developing clinical tools, as well as providing behaviour analytic supervision and consultation to clinical service providers.

Gabriella was born and raised in Miami, FL and is of Salvadoran and Peruvian descent. She obtained a bachelor’s degree in psychology from the University of Florida in 2013 and a masters in human services psychology with an emphasis in applied behaviour analysis from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County in 2016. While she lived in Maryland, she completed her practicum at the Kennedy Krieger Institute, a Johns Hopkins Hospital affiliate, where she gained experience in the assessment and treatment of severe problem behaviour as well as severe feeding disorders.

Following her graduation, she began working as a clinician for Trumpet Behavioral Health (TBH) in Southern California. At TBH, she gained experience in early intervention and skill acquisition for individuals with autism and other developmental disabilities. In August of 2018, Gabriella moved to San Jose, Costa Rica and began working toward raising local awareness of evidence-based treatment for autism and other developmental disabilities. Additionally, she began providing supervision to aspiring behavior analysts in Latin America. In January of 2021, she moved to Panama City, Panama. Gabriella’s recent experiences have made her a passionate advocate for the ethical dissemination of sound behavior analytic therapy. DAXTA’s mission to globally elevate accessibility and quality of behavior analytic treatment falls directly in line with Gabriella’s values and interests to disseminate behavior analysis
